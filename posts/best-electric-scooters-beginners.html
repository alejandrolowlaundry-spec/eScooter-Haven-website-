Best Electric Scooters for Beginners in 2025

February 3, 2025 12 min read Written by Sam Chen

Key Takeaways

  • The Segway Ninebot E2 is our top pick for budget beginners under $350
  • Pneumatic (air-filled) tires are essential for a comfortable, safe ride — avoid solid tires for street use
  • For most beginners, a 350–500W motor and 20–25 mile range is more than enough
  • Always check IP rating before riding in rain — look for at least IPX4
  • Reputable brands with strong warranty support matter more for beginners than raw specs

Buying your first electric scooter should be exciting, not overwhelming. Walk into the market blind and you'll find hundreds of options ranging from $150 throw-aways to $2,000 performance machines — and almost no guidance on what actually matters.

We've been riding, testing, and reviewing electric scooters since 2018. This guide cuts through the noise to give you honest, experience-based recommendations for every budget. These are the scooters we'd buy with our own money.

What to Look for in a Beginner Scooter

Before getting into specific models, let's talk about the specs that genuinely matter for first-time riders — and the ones that are just marketing noise.

Pneumatic Tires (Non-Negotiable)

Solid tires are cheaper to manufacture and never go flat, which is why budget brands love them. But solid tires transmit every crack, pebble, and bump directly through the deck and into your feet. On anything other than a perfectly smooth floor, they're uncomfortable and reduce your control. Pneumatic (air-filled) tires absorb road shock, provide better grip, and give you a far safer ride. If a scooter has solid tires, cross it off your list.

Braking System

A beginner scooter should have two independent braking systems. Look for disc brakes (mechanical or hydraulic) on at least the rear wheel, with electronic regenerative braking on the front. Drum brakes are acceptable but less effective in wet conditions. Avoid scooters with only one braking mechanism.

Motor Power (350–500W is the Sweet Spot)

More watts doesn't always mean better for beginners. A 500W motor handles most urban terrain and slight inclines comfortably at 15–20 mph — which is a safe learning speed. 250W motors struggle on any incline. 1000W+ motors are fast and exciting, but the power delivery can be jerky and unpredictable for new riders.

Real-World Range vs Claimed Range

Manufacturers test range under ideal conditions: 165 lb rider, flat road, 15 mph, 68°F. In reality, expect 20–30% less. A scooter claiming 20 miles will give most riders 14–16 miles. For a typical 5-mile round-trip commute, a 15-mile real range is plenty with buffer to spare.

Warranty and Brand Support

For beginners especially, buy from a brand that will actually support you. Segway/Ninebot and Xiaomi have the best parts availability and customer support networks. No-name brands on Amazon may be $100 cheaper but leave you stranded when something goes wrong after month 3.

Our Top 5 Picks for 2025

1. Segway Ninebot E2 — Best Overall Budget Pick (~$299)

The E2 represents remarkable value for a beginner scooter. It's heavier than some competitors at 26.9 lbs, but the build quality is exceptional for the price — this is a scooter that will last years, not months. The 300W motor handles flat urban terrain with ease, the 9" pneumatic tires absorb bumps well, and the mechanical disc brake provides confident stopping. At 15.5 mph top speed and a real-world range of about 14 miles, it's perfect for shorter commutes.

Best for: City commuters on a tight budget who prioritize build quality over performance.

2. Xiaomi Electric Scooter 4 — Best Design and App Features (~$449)

Xiaomi makes scooters with a clean aesthetic and thoughtful features, and the Electric Scooter 4 is their best entry-level offering yet. The 10" self-repairing tires are a unique feature — small punctures seal themselves, which is a game-changer for commuters. The companion app tracks ride stats, adjusts speed modes, and even displays a lock feature. Real-world range is about 20 miles at moderate speed, and the 500W motor handles mild hills comfortably.

Best for: Tech-oriented beginners who want a stylish scooter with app integration.

3. Apollo Air 2023 — Best for Lighter Riders (~$549)

At just 26 lbs with a 350W motor, the Apollo Air is the most nimble scooter on this list. The semi-hydraulic brakes are exceptional — better than anything else in this price range — and the folding mechanism is one of the easiest to use. The 10" air-filled tires and front suspension provide a genuinely comfortable ride. It's not ideal for heavier riders (max weight 220 lbs) or hilly terrain, but for light urban use, it's outstanding.

Best for: Lighter riders (under 180 lbs) who prioritize handling and portability.

4. Segway Ninebot Max G2 — Best Premium Pick (~$899)

If your budget stretches to $900, the Max G2 is a significant step up from anything below it. The 450W motor (900W peak) handles steep hills without hesitation. Real-world range sits around 35–40 miles — comfortably the longest range in its class. The 10" self-healing tires, hydraulic front/rear brakes, and IPX5 water resistance make this a genuine all-weather commuter. It's heavier at 43 lbs, but the folding mechanism includes a one-step lock that makes carrying it manageable.

Best for: Commuters who want a premium, long-range scooter they'll use daily for years.

5. Hiboy S2 Pro — Best Budget Runner-Up (~$399)

The Hiboy S2 Pro punches above its price with a 500W motor, 10.5" pneumatic tires, and a claimed range of 25 miles (real-world: ~18 miles). Front and rear disc brakes provide solid stopping power, and the large tires make it more comfortable than smaller-wheeled competitors. It's not as well-built as Segway or Xiaomi, but customer service is responsive and spare parts are available. A solid choice if the other options are out of stock.

Best for: Budget buyers who need more power and range than the E2 provides.

Budget Recommendations by Price

  • Under $300: Segway Ninebot E2 is the only brand-name option worth considering. Avoid no-name alternatives — quality control is a serious issue at this price point.
  • $300–$500: The Xiaomi Electric Scooter 4 or Hiboy S2 Pro. The Xiaomi is the better choice if you're buying new; the Hiboy is a solid backup.
  • $500–$700: Apollo Air 2023 for lighter riders, or look for a refurbished/open-box Ninebot Max G1 in this price range.
  • $700–$1,000: The Segway Ninebot Max G2 is the clear winner. Nothing in this range comes close to its combination of range, build quality, and reliability.

What to Avoid as a Beginner

Red Flags When Buying

Avoid scooters with: solid tires of any type; no-name brands with no verifiable warranty; claimed range over 30 miles under $400 (it's not possible with current battery technology); speeds over 25 mph for a first scooter; single-brake systems; no IP water resistance rating.

Frequently Asked Questions

For most beginners, the sweet spot is $300–$500. Below $300, build quality drops sharply and you risk getting a scooter that needs repair within 6 months. Above $500, you start getting meaningful improvements in range, brakes, and ride quality — but it's a lot to invest before you know how much you'll actually ride.

The E2 and Xiaomi 4 are rated IPX5, meaning they can handle rain riding. The Ninebot Max G2 is also IPX5. Avoid riding through standing water with any of these. The Apollo Air has an IP54 rating — fine for light rain but not downpours. Always let the scooter dry before charging.
At minimum: a certified helmet (not just any bike helmet — look for helmets rated for 15+ mph impact). Wrist guards and knee pads are strongly recommended for beginners — wrist fractures are the most common e-scooter injury. Bright clothing or a safety vest is essential for any road riding. Lights (front white, rear red) are legally required in most jurisdictions after dark.
Search "[your city/state] electric scooter laws 2025" — laws change frequently so always check current regulations. In the US, most states allow e-scooters on roads and bike paths under 20 mph without a license. Some cities ban them from sidewalks. In the EU, rules vary dramatically by country. In the UK, only rental e-scooters are currently legal on public roads.
